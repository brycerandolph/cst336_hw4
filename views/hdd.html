Hardware and Construction

HDD stands for hard disk drive. Physical hard drives are non volatile storage devices that utilize a set of magnetic platters to store data. A magnetic head on a moving actuator arm reads the data. Data is stored in a random-access way that individual blocks of data do not need to be adjacent in memory to be related. The are quite suscetiple to damage and data loss as a result of physical shock. They are comparatively cheaper than SSDs.

Transfer Speeds

The transfer speeds on hard disk drives are on average 80MB/s-160MB/s.The most common rotational speeds you will see HDDs run at are 5400rpm, 7200rpm, and 10000rpm. Generally, the lower the rpm, the slower the transfer speeds will be, with a 5400rpm driving having 33% longer average access times of a 7200rpm drive.
